Exam Format

Total Exam time: 2hr 35mins


Section I (Multiple Choice Questions)

55 mins | 55 questions | 40% of score

• Sets of usually 3–4 questions based on the same stimulus.

• Include one or more: primary and secondary texts, images (for example, artwork, photos, posters, cartoons), charts, and maps.



Section II (Free Response Questions) | 1hr 40mins | 5 questions | 60% of score


Section IIA (Short Answer Questions) | 40 mins (recommended) | 3 questions | 20% of score

Focuses on historical developments/processes between 2 time periods:

• Question 1 (required): 1–2 secondary sources. Between the years 1754 and 1980.

• Question 2 (required): 1–2 primary sources. Between the years 1754 and 1980.

• Question 3 [1491 and 1877] and Question 4 [1865 and 2001] (choice of two): no sources included


Section IIB (Document Based Question and Long Essay) | 60 mins (recommended) | 2 questions | 40% of score

(Scoring Details regarding FRQs at the end of the page.)

Document-Based Question | 25% of score

• 1754-1980

• Presented with 7 documents that give various perspectives on a historical development or process

• Develop and support an argument based on these documents and other evidence from your own knowledge.


Long Essay Question | 15% of score

• 3 questions given: 1491 to 1800, 1800 to 1898, 1890 to 2001

• Choice of 3 questions: Pick 1 to answer.

• Documents will not be given; come up with evidence with your prior knowledge.
