APUSH Timeline

1491 – Present

1492

Columbus arrives in the Americas.

1512

Encomienda system established in Spanish colonies

1588

Defeat of the Spanish Armada → rise of English naval power

1607

Founding of Jamestown, first permanent English settlement.

1619

First Africans brought to Virginia (beginning of slavery in English colonies)

1620

Pilgrims land at Plymouth; Mayflower Compact signed.

1630

Massachusetts Bay Colony founded (Puritans)

1676

Bacon’s Rebellion in Virginia

1692

Salem Witch Trials

1730s-1740s

First Great Awakening

1754-1763

Fench and Indian War

1763

Proclamation Line of 1763 (limits westward expansion)

1765

Stamp Act passed → colonial protests

1770

Boston Massacre

1773

Boston Tea Party

1775

Battles of Lexington and Concord (Revolution begins)

1776

Declaration of Independence

1781

Articles of Confederation ratified; Battle of Yorktown (British surrender)

1787

Constitutional Convention → U.S. Constitution drafted

1789

George Washington becomes first president

1791

Bill of Rights ratified.

1794

Whiskey Rebellion

1796

Washington’s Farewell Address

1800

Thomas Jefferson elected

1803

Louisiana Purchase; Marbury v. Madison (judicial review)

1812–1815

War of 1812

1820

Missouri Compromise

1823

Monroe Doctrine

1828

Andrew Jackson elected president

1830

Indian Removal Act

1831

Nat Turner’s Rebellion

1832

Nullification Crisis

1845

Texas annexed

1846–1848

Mexican-American War

1848

Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo; Seneca Falls Convention

1850

Compromise of 1850 (Fugitive Slave Act)

1852

Uncle Tom’s Cabin published

1854

Kansas-Nebraska Act

1857

Dred Scott decision

1860

Abraham Lincoln elected president

1861–1865

Civil War

1863

Emancipation Proclamation

1865

Lincoln assassinated; 13th Amendment abolishes slavery

1868

14th Amendment (citizenship, equal protection)

1870

15th Amendment (voting rights for Black men)

1877

• End of Reconstruction (Compromise of 1877)

• Great Railroad Strike

1882

Chinese Exclusion Act

1890

Sherman Antitrust Act; Wounded Knee Massacre

1892

Homestead Strike

1896

Plessy v. Ferguson (“separate but equal”)

1898

Spanish-American War

1901–1909

Theodore Roosevelt presidency (Progressivism, trust-busting)

1914–1918

World War I (U.S. enters in 1917)

1919

Treaty of Versailles; 18th Amendment (Prohibition)

1920

19th Amendment (women’s suffrage)

1929

Stock Market Crash → Great Depression

1933-1945

FDR presidency; New Deal reforms

1941

Pearl Harbor → U.S. enters WWII

1945

Atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki; WWII ends; UN founded

1947

Truman Doctrine

Marshall Plan (containment of communism)

1954

Brown v. Board of Education (desegregation)

1963

March on Washington; MLK “I Have a Dream” speech; JFK assassinated

1964

Civil Rights Act

1965

Voting Rights Act; U.S. escalates in Vietnam War

1968

MLK and RFK assassinated

1973

Roe v. Wade; U.S. withdraws from Vietnam

1974

Nixon resigns (Watergate Scandle)

1980

Ronald Reagan elected (rise of conservatism)

1991

Cold War ends (collapse of USSR)

2001

9/11 terrorist attacks → War on Terror begins

2008

Barack Obama elected (first African American president)

2020

COVID-19 pandemic

2021

Joe Biden inaugurated; January 6th Capitol riot
