Unit : 1980-Present

Key Concept 9.1: A newly ascendant conservative movement achieved several political and policy goals during the 1980s and continued to strongly influence public discourse in the following decades.

I. The Rise of Conservatism

Reagan Revolution (1981-1989):

  • Election of 1980: Ronald Reagan defeated Jimmy Carter, representing conservative victory
  • Core Beliefs: Limited government, free markets, traditional values, strong military
  • "Government is not the solution to our problem; government is the problem" - Reagan's 1st Inaugural

Conservative Policies:

  • Reaganomics/Supply-Side Economics: Tax cuts (especially for wealthy), deregulation
  • Economic Results: Initial recession, then recovery; tripled national debt
  • Social Issues: Appealed to Christian Right/Religious Right (Moral Majority)

Conservative Coalition:

  • Business interests, anti-communists, religious conservatives, "Reagan Democrats"
  • Neoconservatives: Advocated aggressive foreign policy to spread democracy

II. Continuation of Conservative Influence

  • George H.W. Bush (1989-1993): "Read my lips: no new taxes" - then raised taxes
  • Bill Clinton (1993-2001): "Third Way" Democrat - moved party rightward
    • Welfare Reform (1996): Ended federal entitlement, gave control to states
    • NAFTA (1994): Free trade with Mexico/Canada
  • George W. Bush (2001-2009): Compassionate conservatism, tax cuts, No Child Left Behind
  • Tea Party Movement (2009+): Response to Obama, demanded smaller government
Key Concept 9.2: Moving into the 21st century, the nation experienced significant technological, economic, and demographic changes.

I. Technological Revolution

  • Personal Computing: Microsoft (Bill Gates), Apple (Steve Jobs)
  • Internet Revolution: Changed commerce, communication, information access
  • Social Media: Facebook (2004), Twitter (2006) - transformed politics/society
  • Economic Impact: Dot-com Bubble (1990s boom and 2000 bust)

II. Economic Changes

Globalization:

  • Outsourcing: Manufacturing jobs moved overseas
  • Service Economy: Shift from manufacturing to service jobs
  • Income Inequality: Growing gap between rich and poor

2008 Financial Crisis:

  • Causes: Housing bubble, subprime mortgages, deregulation
  • Effects: Great Recession, bank bailouts, stimulus packages
  • Occupy Wall Street (2011): "We are the 99%" protest movement

III. Demographic Shifts

  • Immigration Changes:
    • Immigration Act of 1990: Increased legal immigration
    • Illegal Immigration: Major political issue, border enforcement debates
    • DACA (2012): Protection for undocumented immigrants brought as children
  • Aging Population: Baby Boomers reaching retirement, straining Social Security/Medicare
  • Sun Belt Growth: Continued migration to South and West
Key Concept 9.3: The end of the Cold War and new challenges to U.S. leadership forced the nation to redefine its foreign policy and role in the world.

I. End of the Cold War

  • Reagan's Role: Military buildup, Strategic Defense Initiative ("Star Wars"), summits with Gorbachev
  • Fall of Berlin Wall (1989): Symbolic end of Cold War
  • Collapse of Soviet Union (1991): U.S. as sole superpower

II. Post-Cold War Foreign Policy

George H.W. Bush:

  • Persian Gulf War (1991): Coalition liberated Kuwait

Bill Clinton:

  • Humanitarian Interventions: Somalia, Bosnia, Kosovo
  • Free Trade Expansion: NAFTA, WTO

September 11, 2001:

  • Al-Qaeda attacks on World Trade Center and Pentagon
  • USA PATRIOT Act: Expanded government surveillance powers
  • Department of Homeland Security created

War on Terror:

  • Afghanistan War (2001+): Target Al-Qaeda and Taliban
  • Iraq War (2003-2011): Controversial WMD justification
  • Debates: Civil liberties vs. security, torture ("enhanced interrogation")

III. 21st Century Challenges

  • Rise of China: Economic competitor, regional power
  • Climate Change: International agreements (Kyoto, Paris)
  • Pandemic: COVID-19 (2020) - global health crisis

Key Social & Cultural Developments

I. Continuing Rights Movements

  • LGBTQ+ Rights:
    • Don't Ask, Don't Tell (1993-2011): Compromise on gays in military
    • Obergefell v. Hodges (2015): Legalized same-sex marriage nationwide
  • Women's Rights: Ongoing debates over workplace equality, #MeToo movement
  • Disability Rights: Americans with Disabilities Act (1990) - landmark civil rights law

II. Political Polarization

  • Increased Partisanship: Widening gap between Democrats and Republicans
  • Media Fragmentation: Rise of partisan news (Fox News, MSNBC)
  • Impeachment Politics: Clinton (1998), Trump (2019, 2021)
  • Election Controversies: 2000 (Bush v. Gore), 2020 election claims

III. Demographic & Cultural Debates

  • Multiculturalism: Debates over immigration, bilingual education
  • Affirmative Action: Ongoing legal challenges
  • "Culture Wars": Battles over abortion, gun rights, religion in public life
