Unit : 1980-Present

Key Concept 9.1: A newly ascendant conservative movement achieved several political and policy goals during the 1980s and continued to strongly influence public discourse in the following decades.

I. The Rise of Conservatism

Reagan Revolution (1981-1989):

  • Election of 1980: Ronald Reagan defeated Jimmy Carter, representing conservative victory
  • Core Beliefs: Limited government, free markets, traditional values, strong military
  • "Government is not the solution to our problem; government is the problem" - Reagan's 1st Inaugural

Conservative Policies:

  • Reaganomics/Supply-Side Economics: Tax cuts (especially for wealthy), deregulation
  • Economic Results: Initial recession, then recovery; tripled national debt
  • Social Issues: Appealed to Christian Right/Religious Right (Moral Majority)

Conservative Coalition:

  • Business interests, anti-communists, religious conservatives, "Reagan Democrats"
  • Neoconservatives: Advocated aggressive foreign policy to spread democracy

II. Continuation of Conservative Influence

  • George H.W. Bush (1989-1993): "Read my lips: no new taxes" - then raised taxes
  • Bill Clinton (1993-2001): "Third Way" Democrat - moved party rightward
    • Welfare Reform (1996): Ended federal entitlement, gave control to states
    • NAFTA (1994): Free trade with Mexico/Canada
  • George W. Bush (2001-2009): Compassionate conservatism, tax cuts, No Child Left Behind
  • Tea Party Movement (2009+): Response to Obama, demanded smaller government
Key Concept 9.2: Moving into the 21st century, the nation experienced significant technological, economic, and demographic changes.
